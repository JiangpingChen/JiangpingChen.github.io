Misinformation on Social Media

 

 

In today’s digital age, information is constantly generated and distributed through a variety of channels, such as emails, online communities, social media, and blogs. The ease of creating and sharing content has accelerated the spread of misinformation, disinformation, and fake news. Individuals or groups may intentionally produce and circulate false to advance specific agendas.

 

Misinformation can lead to significant and far-reaching consequences, impacting individuals, communities, institutions, and entire societies. As a result, misinformation research has attracted substantial attention across disciplines. This work is critically important to minimize harms, protect public health, safeguard democracy, and promote information literacy. Information scientists, educators, and information professionals are uniquely positioned to examine the effects of misinformation, offer guidance and tools to evaluate information, and improve access to quality information.

 

Our research focuses on understanding the dynamics of misinformation on social media and digital platforms. For examples, what are the patterns of misinformation diffusion on platforms like X (twitter), Facebook, YouTube, and TikTok? How does misinformation spread differently across platforms or cultural contexts? How do people respond to misinformation in different languages and formats?  We may also investigate domain-specific misinformation and explore innovative approaches to enhance information literacy education using real-world misinformation case studies.
